## What this is for

Telegram Business lets a personal account add a chatbot that can read and reply to the account's
DMs on the owner's behalf. Humain uses this so a business owner's personal Telegram account can
auto-answer customers with a kiosk's persona, knowledge base, and guardrails — without a
separate business number or a group chat.

This is different from [group AI](/telegram/bot#connecting-a-group-to-a-kiosk): a Business
connection operates on the owner's **own DMs**, not a group the bot is added to.

---

## Two independent pieces of configuration

A Business connection has two parts that are configured separately and stored in the same
database row, keyed by the owner's Telegram user ID:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Bind a kiosk (in the bot, /secretary)">
    DM the bot privately and run `/secretary`. The bot lists devices in your active workspace —
    selecting one records the workspace, kiosk, and device to use, but leaves replies **disabled**
    until step 2 is also done.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Telegram Business (in Telegram settings, not the bot)">
    In the Telegram app: **Settings → Telegram Business → Chatbots**, select this bot, and grant
    it permission to reply to messages. Telegram then sends the bot a `business_connection`
    event recording the connection ID and reply permission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  These two steps are independent by design — reconnecting Telegram Business (step 2) never
  loses your `/secretary` kiosk binding (step 1), and re-running `/secretary` never disables an
  active Business connection.
</Tip>

Both steps must be complete — kiosk bound **and** Business Mode enabled with reply permission —
before the AI will respond to anything.

## Message handling

<Warning>
  If the connection is only partially configured, incoming Business messages are **dropped
  silently** — no error, no hint is sent anywhere. If your secretary isn't replying, re-check
  both halves of the setup above rather than waiting for an error message.
</Warning>

When a fully-configured Business message arrives:

- **Messages from your own account** are ignored **unless you explicitly @mention the bot** —
  this lets you test the secretary in your own chats without every message you send triggering
  a reply.
- **Messages from your customers** (anyone else in the conversation) are always answered.
- The AI turn runs through the same pipeline as every other channel — retrieval, guardrails,
  persona, tool calls — scoped to that specific business connection and chat.
- The reply is sent **on behalf of the business account** (using Telegram's
  `business_connection_id` parameter on `sendMessage`), so customers see it as coming from you,
  not from a separate bot identity.

## Managed (child) bots

Separately from Business connections, Telegram can grant the Humain bot management rights over
a **child bot** (a `managed_bot` update). When that happens, Humain:

1. Encrypts and stores the child bot's token (AES-256-GCM, `ENCRYPTION_KEY`).
2. Generates a per-bot webhook secret and registers a dedicated webhook for the child bot.
3. Routes the child bot's own updates through the same AI turn pipeline once a workspace is
   bound to it.

<Note>
  A newly granted managed bot has **no workspace bound automatically** — Telegram's
  `managed_bot` update doesn't include an owner or workspace. Until a workspace binding is wired
  up for it, the child bot tells users to have the owner run `/secretary`. Contact your workspace
  admin if you expect a managed bot to already be answering and it isn't.
</Note>

## What you're configuring, in plain terms

| You do this | Where |
|---|---|
| Link your Telegram account to Humain | Admin panel or bot `/start` |
| Bind a kiosk to your account | Bot, private chat, `/secretary` |
| Turn on Business Mode and grant reply permission | Telegram app → Settings → Business → Chatbots |
| Test it | @mention the bot in a chat with yourself, or have a real customer message you |
