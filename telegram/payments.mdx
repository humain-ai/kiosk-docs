## Overview

Telegram Stars (currency code `XTR`) is Telegram's in-app payment system. Humain uses it to sell
two kinds of purchases directly inside Telegram, with no separate checkout page:

- **Subscription** — a recurring monthly plan (`subscription_period` of 2,592,000 seconds / 30
  days).
- **PAYG (pay-as-you-go)** — a one-off credit bundle that adds extra session capacity.

A plain **product** purchase kind also exists but is only recorded for reference — it isn't
wired into workspace billing the way `subscription` and `payg` are.

---

## Where the invoice is created

Invoice creation happens in the **Mini App / `cmd/server`**, not in the bot itself:

1. The Mini App's `/c/pay` compact view (opened via a bot deep link) collects the plan or bundle
   choice and POSTs `{ workspace_id, plan_id | bundle_id }` to
   `/api/v1/telegram/stars/invoice`.
2. `cmd/server` verifies the caller's workspace membership, calls Telegram's
   `createInvoiceLink` API, and returns the resulting `invoice_link`.
3. The Mini App calls `window.Telegram.WebApp.openInvoice(invoice_link, callback)`, which opens
   Telegram's native payment sheet.

The invoice payload is JSON (kept under Telegram's 128-byte limit) containing `user_id`,
`plan_id` or `bundle_id`, `workspace_id`, and `kind` (`subscription`, `product`, or `payg`).

---

## Where the payment is fulfilled

Fulfillment happens in the **bot** (`cmd/telegram`), which is the process that receives
Telegram's payment webhooks:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pre-checkout">
    Telegram sends a pre-checkout query before charging the user. The bot must answer within 10
    seconds — it decodes the invoice payload, verifies `payload.user_id` matches the paying
    user, and accepts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Successful payment">
    On success, Telegram sends a `successful_payment` update. The bot:
    - Writes a row to `telegram_subscriptions`, keyed on `charge_id` (unique) so a duplicate
      webhook delivery is a no-op (`ON CONFLICT charge_id DO NOTHING`).
    - For `subscription` and `payg` kinds, calls into billing to assign the plan or add PAYG
      session credit (`extra_seconds`) to the workspace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Fulfillment is idempotent on `charge_id` — Telegram's at-least-once delivery for payment updates
cannot double-charge or double-fulfill a purchase.

## Data model

`telegram_subscriptions` stores every Stars purchase:

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `telegram_user_id` | The paying Telegram user. |
| `user_id` | The linked Humain user. |
| `plan_id` | Which plan or bundle was purchased. |
| `charge_id` | Telegram's unique charge identifier — the idempotency key. |
| `currency` | Always `XTR`. |
| `amount` | Stars amount charged. |
| `subscription_period` | Seconds for a recurring plan; `null` for a one-off purchase. |
| `status` | `active`, `cancelled`, or `expired`. |
| `current_period_end` | When a recurring plan's current period ends. |

## Testing

Use an `hk_test_` sandbox-linked workspace where possible, but note **Stars payments always use
real Telegram Stars** — there is no sandbox currency for Stars purchases. Test with a small
Stars balance and Telegram's own refund tools if you need to reverse a test transaction.
