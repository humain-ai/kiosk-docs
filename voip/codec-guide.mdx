The Humain SIP gateway accepts audio in several codecs and transcodes internally to Opus before
passing audio to the AI pipeline. Codec choice affects transcription accuracy, bandwidth usage,
and compatibility with your PBX or telephony provider.

---

## Compatibility matrix

| Codec | Humain support | Sample rate | Bandwidth | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| G.711 μ-law (PCMU) | ✅ Supported | 8 kHz | ~64 kbps | Universal — works with every PBX and carrier |
| G.711 A-law (PCMA) | ✅ Supported | 8 kHz | ~64 kbps | European variant of G.711 |
| G.722 | ✅ Supported | 16 kHz | ~64 kbps | Wideband — significantly better STT accuracy |
| Opus | ✅ Supported | 8–48 kHz | 6–510 kbps | Best quality; requires modern SIP stack |
| G.729 | ❌ Not supported | 8 kHz | ~8 kbps | Patent-encumbered; not decoded by gateway |
| G.726 | ❌ Not supported | 8 kHz | ~16–40 kbps | ADPCM variant; not decoded by gateway |
| iLBC | ❌ Not supported | 8 kHz | ~15 kbps | Not decoded by gateway |

## Which codec should I use?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use G.722 if your PBX supports it (recommended for most deployments)">
    G.722 (wideband) doubles the audio bandwidth compared to G.711 narrowband. Deepgram's
    speech-to-text model performs measurably better at 16 kHz, especially for:

    - Non-native English speakers
    - Noisy environments (call centres, drive-throughs)
    - Short commands or proper nouns

    G.722 uses the same ~64 kbps bandwidth as G.711, so there is no cost to enabling it.
    Most modern IP phones, Asterisk/FreeSWITCH, and 3CX support G.722 out of the box.

    **Asterisk PJSIP config:**
    ```ini
    [humain-endpoint]
    ; Prefer G.722, fall back to PCMU/PCMA
    allow=!all,g722,ulaw,alaw
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use Opus for the best quality (WebRTC-native, variable bitrate)">
    Opus is the native codec for WebRTC and operates at up to 48 kHz stereo. It supports
    variable bitrate, making it efficient on constrained links while delivering high quality
    when bandwidth is available.

    Use Opus when:
    - Your PBX or SIP stack supports it (FreeSWITCH, Kamailio, recent Asterisk builds)
    - You are using a WebRTC-to-SIP gateway
    - You want the lowest possible word-error rate from Deepgram

    **Note:** Opus requires the `opus` codec module in Asterisk (`codec_opus`). This module
    is not bundled with Asterisk and must be installed separately from Digium or Sangoma.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use G.711 for maximum compatibility">
    G.711 PCMU (μ-law, North America) and PCMA (A-law, Europe/Rest of World) are supported
    by every SIP device made in the last 30 years. Use G.711 when:

    - You are unsure what codecs your carrier or PBX supports
    - You are connecting legacy ATAs or analogue gateways
    - You are troubleshooting — G.711 eliminates codec negotiation as a variable

    The STT accuracy impact of G.711 is acceptable for clear, quiet audio. In noisy environments
    or with accented speech, consider switching to G.722 if your PBX supports it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="G.729 — why it is not supported">
    G.729 is a low-bitrate (~8 kbps) narrowband codec that requires a licensed decoder. The
    Humain gateway does not include a G.729 license. If your carrier sends G.729, you must
    transcode it to G.711 or G.722 at your PBX before routing to Humain.

    **Asterisk transcode config:**
    ```ini
    ; Transcode G.729 to G.711 at the PBX before sending to Humain
    [outbound-to-humain]
    allow=!all,ulaw,alaw,g722  ; Do NOT include g729 here
    ```

    FreeSWITCH and most hosted PBXes can transcode G.729 to G.711 transparently.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## SDP negotiation

The Humain gateway advertises codecs in this preference order in its SDP `m=audio` line:

```
m=audio 10000 RTP/SAVP 9 0 8 111
a=rtpmap:9   G722/8000
a=rtpmap:0   PCMU/8000
a=rtpmap:8   PCMA/8000
a=rtpmap:111 opus/48000/2
a=fmtp:111   minptime=10;useinbandfec=1
```

Your PBX will select the first mutually supported codec from this list. If you want to force
a specific codec, configure your PBX endpoint to offer only that codec.

## DTMF

The gateway accepts DTMF via **RFC 2833 / RFC 4733** (telephone-event payload type 101). In-band
DTMF (audio tones) is not detected — configure your PBX to use out-of-band DTMF.

**Asterisk:**
```ini
[humain-endpoint]
dtmf_mode=rfc4733
```

**FreeSWITCH:**
```xml
<param name="dtmf-type" value="rfc2833"/>
```
